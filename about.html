What’s a Rich Text element?

The rich text element allows you to create and format headings, paragraphs, blockquotes, images, and video all in one place instead of having to add and format them individually. Just double-click and easily create content.

Static and dynamic content editing

A rich text element can be used with static or dynamic content. For static content, just drop it into any page and begin editing. For dynamic content, add a rich text field to any collection and then connect a rich text element to that field in the settings panel. Voila!

How to customize formatting for each rich text

Headings, paragraphs, blockquotes, figures, images, and figure captions can all be styled after a class is added to the rich text element using the "When inside of" nested selector system.

About OUR MOVEMENT

FIGHTING A CARTEL isn’t easy But  Together we can achieve  positive social change.

Local campaigns with a global impact

One report at a time, Justice for Myanmar and its allies are collecting evidence and exposing the vast business network funding brutal oppression in Myanmar. The campaign aims to pressure individual businesses and investors from countries around the world to divest from Myanmar and deprive the military of profits they receive from every day people like you.


There are 150+ international and domestic companies engaged with military-owned businesses in Myanmar. These businesses run a high risk of helping to fund crimes against humanity. We're going after them one-by-one and already seeing our pressure campaign succeed.

There are 150+ international and domestic companies engaged with military-owned businesses in Myanmar. These businesses run a high risk of helping to fund crimes against humanity. We're going after them one-by-one.

  • Belgian satellite communications firm Newtec cut commercial ties with the Tatmadaw after being exposed in the UN Fact Finding Mission Report.
  • Japanese beverage company Kirin Holdings announced an internal investigation into their military financing after being exposed operating joint ventures with the Tatmadaw, which likely contribute to genocide and crimes against humanity
  • Financial services company Western Union ended its contract with Myawaddy Bank, the 4th largest bank in Myanmar, which is owned by a large military conglomerate.
  • Clothing brand Esprit will no longer source garments from a factory located in an industrial park owned and controlled by the military.

Our plan to push for a military under democratic control

The Myanmar military has built immense wealth through partnerships with domestic and international businesses. This wealth fuels crimes against humanity and we want to disrupt that.
‍
This can only succeed through a global network of activists putting local pressure on a global problem. Here's how it works:‍‍

1. Expose international businesses with financial ties to Myanmar’s military with the release of sourced evidence
2. Disrupt the military’s ability to profit from these ties with boycotts, protests, and other action on the ground
3. Pressure businesses to divest or otherwise take action with the continued sourcing and leaking of new evidence
